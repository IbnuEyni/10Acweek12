# Letter to Future Amir — January 2027

**Written:** July 2025  
**To be read:** January 2027

---

Amir,

Six months ago you were finishing your degree, leading backend at NextGen, and in the middle of the most intense 12 weeks of your life — shipping one production system per week at 10 Academy. You were tired. You were proud. You weren't sure what came next.

So let me ask you what I couldn't answer then:

---

**Are you proud of how far you've come?**

I hope so. Because from where I'm standing — yes. A year ago you were building Flutter apps and integrating CRMs. Now you've shipped event-sourced audit systems, multi-agent courtrooms, evaluation benchmarks published on HuggingFace, and competed on a real ML leaderboard. You went from "I use AI APIs" to "I build the infrastructure AI runs on." That's not a small shift. That's a different engineer.

But I know you. You probably don't feel it yet. You're already looking at the next thing. So I'm telling you from the past: stop for a second and acknowledge the distance.

---

**Did you land the opportunity you were chasing?**

I don't know what you landed. But I know what you were chasing: a Forward Deployed Engineer role where you build real systems for real companies — not maintenance work, not CRUD, not "AI wrappers." Something where the problems are hard and the work matters.

If you got it — you earned it. 9 systems in 12 weeks. A published benchmark. A team that trusted you to drive.

If you didn't — you're still the person who built all of that. The opportunity didn't define you before. It doesn't now.

---

**What did you build after the bootcamp?**

This is the question I'm most curious about. The bootcamp gave you structure — a new system every week, a pair partner, deadlines. Without that structure, did you keep building? Did you turn Oracle Forge into something real? Did the Ledger pattern show up in a production system somewhere? Did you write more?

I hope you didn't stop. The momentum is the hardest thing to rebuild.

---

**What habits did you start, stop, or continue?**

**Start:**
- Writing publicly (Medium, Substack). You have things to say. The article about 9 systems in 12 weeks — did you publish it? Did you keep going?
- Committing daily. Not for the green graph. For the compound effect.

**Stop:**
- Building in silence. You spent too long with great work and no visibility. LinkedIn, GitHub, Medium — the profile polish wasn't vanity. It was making your work findable.
- Saying yes to everything. You were doing NextGen + A2SV + 10 Academy + side projects. Something had to give. I hope you chose intentionally.

**Continue:**
- Going deep. The Ledger wasn't a tutorial project. 166 tests. Cryptographic audit chains. That depth is what separates you.
- Pairing. Ephrata, Nebiyou, Ruth, Abdurahim — the team made you sharper. Keep finding people who push you.

---

**What kind of person are you becoming?**

Someone who builds systems, not features. Someone who thinks in invariants, not just implementations. Someone who can explain the "why" behind the "what" — to a partner, to a recruiter, to a future teammate.

You're becoming an engineer who ships. Not plans. Not designs. Ships.

Keep going.

— Amir, July 2025
